My Story

I am a Spring 2022 graduate from The University of Houston with a Bachelor of Business Administration in Management Information Systems and the distinction of Summa Cum Laude (3.98 GPA). I began my career as a Cloud Analyst on the Security and Controls team at Oracle Corporation in Austin, TX where I maintained well-above the satisfactory utilization percentage as a consultant and took on my first professional coding projects in the form of python scripting to automate tedious consulting tasks both for clients and internally. Moving forward, I am blessed to be joining Capital One Financial's Capital One Developer Academy (CODA) as an associate to work towards becoming a Software Engineer in their Technology Development Program. I was also recently accepted into Georgia Tech's Master of Science - Analytics Program where I will be building a Data Science focused skillset part-time on evenings and weekends. I've continued to spend countless hours watching YouTube tutorials about programming, using freeCodeCamp and other similar resources, and practicing on CodeWars.com to become a better programmer. I am so excited about my future as a Software Engineer, and once again I am really thankful that you've taken the time to follow my journey. I am incredibly excited to document my progress here, and look back in the future.

In high school, I really found my people within my Pre-AP and AP Computer Science courses who were okay with being nerdy and excited about tech, which was a welcome break from the lack of camaraderie I found on our school's basketball team. That interest in computer science persisted, but I also had a strong interest in learning about business due to idolizing successful tech entrepreneurs I had read about my entire life. This interest in both business and computer science led me to pursue a degree in Management Information Systems which seemed to be a great combination of both. During my time as an upperclassman at UH, I took introductory courses covering Java, SQL, Python, R, HTML/CSS, JavaScript and other technical topics and I fell in love with those courses. My professors were incredibly interesting, learning the ins-and-outs of computers was fascinating, and I really just wanted to know more. I graduated feeling like I had a baseline understanding, but knew how dedicated I was to mastering the concepts I'd only grazed over through my undergraduate coursework.

So that's where I am now. I'm dead set on becoming a world-class Software Engineer, and I'm dedicating my free time during evenings and on weekends to achieve my lofty ambitions and anticipating starting my career as a Software Engineer at Capital One.
